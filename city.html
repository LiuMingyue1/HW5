Some Interesting Places

Panda Base - A Sanctuary for China's Iconic Giant Pandas
The Panda Base, officially known as the Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding, is a world-renowned conservation center located in the heart of Sichuan province, China. Established in 1987, this remarkable facility has played a pivotal role in the preservation and protection of the iconic giant panda species, which is native to China and cherished worldwide.
Spanning over 500 acres of lush bamboo forests and meticulously designed enclosures, the Panda Base serves as both a research center and a sanctuary for these beloved black-and-white bears. Here, visitors have the extraordinary opportunity to observe giant pandas in a semi-natural habitat, gaining insights into their behavior, biology, and ongoing conservation efforts to save them from the brink of extinction.

Jinsha Site - Uncovering the Mysteries of Ancient China
The Jinsha Site is an archaeological marvel that offers a captivating glimpse into China's ancient history. Dating back over 3,000 years, this significant discovery unveils a rich civilization with intricate artifacts, including exquisite gold, bronze, and jade relics. The site's historical importance lies in its role as a pivotal ceremonial center of the ancient Shu Kingdom. Visitors can explore the meticulously preserved ruins, gaining insights into the culture, art, and spirituality of this ancient civilization. Jinsha Site stands as a testament to the enduring legacy of China's past, attracting history enthusiasts and archaeologists from around the world.

Wuhou Shrine - Honoring Ancient Heroes
The Wuhou Shrine is a revered historical site paying homage to the renowned Three Kingdoms era figures, including the accomplished strategist Zhuge Liang and valiant generals Liu Bei and Guan Yu. Established as a tribute to their remarkable contributions, the shrine's serene ambiance features splendid architectural structures, ancient trees, and traditional Chinese gardens. Visitors can immerse themselves in the rich history of the Three Kingdoms period, appreciating the cultural significance and reverence associated with these legendary characters. Wuhou Shrine remains a cherished destination for history enthusiasts and a symbol of admiration for these iconic figures from ancient Chinese history.

Wenshu Monastery - A Tranquil Oasis in Chengdu
Wenshu Monastery is a serene and ancient Buddhist temple dating back to the Tang Dynasty. Known as a spiritual sanctuary in the bustling city, it offers a peaceful retreat for both worshippers and visitors. The monastery's architecture is a masterpiece of Chinese temple design, featuring intricate wooden carvings, stunning gardens, and tranquil courtyards. Inside, you'll find a vast collection of Buddhist scriptures, relics, and art pieces, providing a glimpse into China's rich religious heritage. Wenshu Monastery is not only a cultural gem but also a place of meditation and reflection, making it a must-visit destination in Chengdu.

Taikoo Li Chengdu - Where Tradition Meets Modernity
Taikoo Li Chengdu, often referred to simply as Taikoo Li, is a vibrant and trendy lifestyle and shopping district nestled in the heart of Chengdu, Sichuan Province. This urban oasis seamlessly blends traditional Sichuan architecture with modern design, creating a captivating atmosphere. Visitors can explore a diverse range of high-end boutiques, restaurants, cafes, and cultural spaces amidst picturesque courtyards and traditional teahouses. Taikoo Li is not just a shopping destination but also a cultural hub where East meets West, offering a unique fusion of old-world charm and contemporary sophistication. It's a must-visit spot for those seeking a taste of Chengdu's modern cosmopolitan lifestyle.

One Famous Restaurant

Ma Wang Zi
Established in 1923, Ma Wang Zi Restaurant boasts a century of culinary heritage in Chengdu, renowned as one of the city's most revered Sichuan cuisine establishments. The restaurant's skilled chefs ensure each dish retains its original flavors while infusing the unique Sichuan spice and aroma.
The ambiance at Ma Wang Zi is welcoming and comfortable, with attentive staff providing a home-away-from-home experience. Whether it's a family gathering, a friendly get-together, or a business banquet, Ma Wang Zi Restaurant is the ideal dining destination. It not only represents Chengdu's rich culinary traditions but also carries a century of history and culture, offering diners an unforgettable journey into the world of Sichuan cuisine.

One 6 Days Trip

  1. Day 1: Arrive
    1. Arrive in Chengdu
    2. Check-in to hotel
    3. Enjoy Sichuan cuisine at a restaurant near our hotel in the evening

  2. Day 2: Jinsha Site and Panda Base
    1. Morning visit to the Jinsha Site to learn about the history and culture of the ancient Shu Kingdom
    2. Have lunch at a nearby restaurant
    3. Afternoon trip to the Chengdu Panda Base to observe adorable giant pandas
    4. Return to the city in the evening for a hotpot dinner

  3. Day 3: Wuhou Shrine and Jinli Ancient Street
    1. Morning visit to Wuhou Shrine to explore the history of the Three Kingdoms and the stories of Liu Bei and Zhuge Liang
    2. Lunch at a traditional Sichuan restaurant near Wuhou Shrine
    3. Afternoon stroll through Jinli Ancient Street for shopping and local snacks
    4. Evening dinner at a restaurant near Jinli Ancient Street

  4. Day 4: Wenshu Monastery and Du Fu Thatched Cottage
    1. Morning visit to Wenshu Monastery to appreciate the serene temple and Buddhist culture
    2. Enjoy lunch at a restaurant nearby
    3. Afternoon visit to Du Fu Thatched Cottage to learn about the life and works of the ancient Chinese poet Du Fu
    4. Evening free time to explore the city or dine at a local restaurant of your choice

  5. Day 5: Taikoo Li and Kuanzhai Alley
    1. Morning trip to Taikoo Li to admire the blend of modern and classical architecture and culture
    2. Lunch at a restaurant within Taikoo Li
    3. Afternoon exploration of Kuanzhai Alley to experience the old Chengdu charm and local customs
    4. Evening enjoy Chengdu street food in Kuanzhai Alley

  6. Day 6: Free Time and Departure
    1. Morning free time for optional activities, exploring other Chengdu attractions, or shopping
    2. Have last Sichuan cuisine meal in the city at noon
    3. Depart Chengdu in the afternoon
